White Space

ESPN

Overall I think ESPN uses whitespace very well on there site. It really helps you focus on whatever part of the page you want to look at. On the page they have words, numbers, and images all of various sizes. The white space allows you to see clearly the score to a game(large font), the teams names(medium font), and how much time is left in the game(small font).

Alignment

Fox News

With not a lot of content on the screen Fox News does a pretty good job of making small alignment changes to help the content seperate from other content. The images take up the whole width of the screen, but they indent and align the caption for the image and the header that shows a hierarcy. Then they indent the article links a little more, same amount as header from the edge, which causes your eyes to follow the indentation path to the article links and then hopefully click on a link and stay on their site.

Hicks Law

Apple

For a company that makes the money Apple does and holds so much market share on phones and mobile devies, you don't have to choose a lot from there main page. They put a new product first with links to learn more about it, but when you click on the menu in the upper left corner you are given an option to choose any of there products. They also have a link to shop on there site, but it doesn't take from displaying a product. As you scroll down he screen they continue with one product on the screen most of the way down.
